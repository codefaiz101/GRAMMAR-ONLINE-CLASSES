Gender
  • The nouns used for males (humans and animals)are categorised as masculine gender.

    • Examples: man, father, son, uncle, brother, tiger, lion, peacock.



  • The nouns used for females (humans and animals) are categorised as feminine gender.

    • Examples: women, mother, daughter, aunt, sister, tigress, lioness, peahen.



  • The nouns used for both masculine and feminine genders are categorised as common gender

    • Examples: baby, friend, nurse, doctor, artist, teacher.



  • The nouns that are neither masculine nor feminine are categorised as neuter gender.

    • Examples: bench, pencil, room.



    Example sentense:
  • The noun 'boy' is male and belongs to the masculine gender.
  • The noun 'girl' is female and belongs to the feminine gender.
  • The noun 'student' can refer to a boy or a girl and belongs to the common gender.
  • The noun 'bench' cannot be classified as male or female. It belongs to the neuter gender.
