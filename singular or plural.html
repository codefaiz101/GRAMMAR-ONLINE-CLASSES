Singular and plural

Singular nouns tell us about one person, place, animal or thing.
Plural nouns tell us about two or more people , places, animals or things. We usually add -s or -es to a singular noun to make it plural.

  • cat : cats
  • potato : potatoes
  • box : boxes

    • Some plurals are formed by adding -ies or -vesto singular nouns.

  • fly : flies
  • wolf : wolves

    • plurals can be formed by changing vowels in the singular nouns.

  • man : men
  • foot : feet

    • We can also add -en or -ren to singular nouns.

  • ox : oxen
  • child : children

    • Some nouns have the same form of singular and plural.

  • sheep : sheep
  • deer : deer

    • Some plural nouns are completely different from the singular nouns.

  • mouse : mice

    • Some nouns have only plural forms. They are never used as singular nouns.

  • clothes
  • scissors
  • stairs
