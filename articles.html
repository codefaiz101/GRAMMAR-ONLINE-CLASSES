Articles

Aand an calledindefinite articles because they do not point out to any particular person or thing. We use 'a' or 'an' with a singular noun.

We use a or an only before singular nouns.

  • a boy
  • an orange

    • We use a before singular nouns which begin with consonant sounds

  • a dog
  • a tree

    • We use an with singular nouns which begin withvowel siunds such as a, e, i, o, u.

  • an eraser
  • an egg

    • When adjective are used before singular nouns, we use a before adjectives that begin with consonant sounds and an before adjectives.that begin with vowel sounds.

  • a tall boy
  • an obedient boy

    • We use the before some proper nouns such as the names of oceans, rivers, and mountain ranges.

  • Once upon a time there were six mice in a house. There was plenty of food in the house.

    • We use the when there is only one such thing.

  • When you look at the sky at night
