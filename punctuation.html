Punctuation

Punctuation is a set of marks that regulates and clarifies the meanings of different texts. The term “punctuation” has originated from the Medieval Latin word “pūnctuātiōn,” which means markings or signs.
symbols or marks are called 'punctuation marks' or 'punctuation' .

    full stop

we put a full stop (.) at the end of a sentence.

Example:
  • we saw a turtle on the beach.
  • It was basking in the sun.

      • Capitilization

    we use capital letters.

  • to start a new sentence.

    • Example:
  • Ladybugs are a type of a bettle.They come in many different colours and patterns.
  • for proper nouns (names of persons,countries, places, months, days of the week,etc.)

    • Example:
  • Sumi went to Sri lanka with Hari. They reached Kandy on Sunday.
  • I am a good boy.
      • Comma

    we use commas to

  • separate items in a list.

    • Example:

    1)There were cack, cjips, chocolate and lemonade on the table.

  • separate adjective, when more than one adjective is used to describe something.

    • Example:

    she was a shy ,gentle, and soft-spoken girl.

      Question Mark

    we put a Question mark(?) at the end of a question.

    Example:

  • who discovered sugarcane?
  • where was it grown?
    1. Exclamation Mark

    we use an exclamation mark(!) to show emotion (surprise, excitement,anger).

  • oh no! That is bad news.
  • Hurrah! Rohit has won the race.
  • Be quite! Do not talk so much.

      • Apostophe

    we use apostrophe
    we add an 's after a singular name or noun.
    we added an' after a plural name or noun.

  • That is Rohan's pet dog.
  • The dog's coat is brown and soft.
  • This is Charl's storybook.
