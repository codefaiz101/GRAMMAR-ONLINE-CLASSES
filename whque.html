wh questions

The WH- QUESTIONS are questions formed with a question word, such as who, whom, whose, what, which, when, where, why or how. These question words are also called INTERROGATIVE WORDS.

Wh- questions can be categorized according to the purpose they serve for an asker. Basically, there are three types:

  • information questions,
  • repeat please questions, and
  • elaborate please questions.

    • Information wh- questions are the basic type. They are used to request information that has not been previously mentioned, and they take the different forms considered thus far.

    Unlike yes/no questions, wh- information questions always contain a presupposition. For instance, when we ask the question What did John do? we presume that John did in fact do something.

    Question: 1) How did they do it?
    Presumed Information:They did it somehow.

    Question: 2) Where did John go?
    Presumed Information: John went somewhere.

    Question: 3) Whose book was stolen?
    Presumed Information: Someone’s book was stolen.

    Question:4) What has he done?
    Presumed Information: He has done something.

    Question:5) Who is doing it?
    Presumed Information: Someone is doing it.

    Question words: What
    Usages:Used to ask about things
    Examples: - What are you doing?
    - What do you think about the movie?

    Question words:When
    Usages:Used to ask about time
    Examples: - When will the meeting start?
    - When are you leaving?

    Question words:Where
    Usages:Used to ask about places
    Examples:
    - Where’s my bag? - Where do you live?

    Question words:Who
    Usages: Used to ask about people
    Examples:
    - Who do you love the most in your family?
    - Who told you that story?

    Question words:Whom
    Usages: Used to ask about people (object of verb)
    Examples:
    - Whom did you see in the morning? I saw Mr. Mark, my English teacher.
    - Whom was Jim talking to? He was talking to Jack, his new roommate.


    Question words:Which
    Usages: Used to ask about choices
    Examples:
    - Which one do you choose? The left or right?
    - Of all the drinks in the menu, which one would you like?

    Question words:Whose
    Usages: Used to ask about possession
    Examples:
    - Whose pencil is this? Is it yours? - Whose books are these?


    Question words:Why
    Usages: Used to ask about reasons/ causes
    Examples:
    - Why did it happen? I didn’t understand.
    - Why is he crying?

    Question words:How
    Usages: Used to ask about manner/ process
    Examples:
    - How can you explain this problem? Please tell us.
    - How can you get here?
