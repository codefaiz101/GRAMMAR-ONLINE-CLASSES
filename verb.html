Verb

The verb is very important in english. Verbs constitute one of the main parts of speech in the English language. Like other type of words in the language, English verbs are not heavily inflected. Most combinations of tense, aspect, mood and voice are expressed periphrastically, using constructions with auxiliary verbs.

Words such as 'are', 'go', 'finishes', 'goes', 'helps', 'has', are called 'verbs'.

Verbs are action words:
  • what someone or something has
    • Example:
  • Mary has a little lamb.
  • what something or someone is
    • Example:
  • Mary and jim are friends.
  • Mary is hardworking.
  • what someone or something does.

    • Example:

  • They go to the same school.
  • Mary helps her classmate with their work.

    • Every sentence has a subject and a verb.
    The subject is generally the person or the thing that the sentence is about . It is often the person or the thing that performs the action.
