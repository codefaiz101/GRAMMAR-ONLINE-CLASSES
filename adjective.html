Adjectives

The words that describe the nouns are called 'adjectives'.

  • The fruit has many yellow bananas in his cart.
  • The fruit seller gave me seven sweet bananas.

    • The words many and seven indicate how many bananas(noun).
    These are called adjective of Quantity. The word yellow and sweet indicate what kind of bananas. These are called adjective of Quality.



    Descriptive Adjectives

    A descriptive adjective is an adjective that modifies a noun or pronoun by describing it or expressing its quality.

    For example:


  • In these sentences, we have placed the adjectives before the nouns.

    • a pretty doll
    pretty=adjective
    doll= noun


  • we can also place the adjective after the verb.

    • The doll is pretty.
    is = verb
    pretty= adjective
    The pen is expensive.
    is =verb
    expensive= adjective

    Descriptive adjective tell us more about the size(big, large) shape(square), colour(red, pink)and quality (pretty,soft)of a noun.

    Quantity Adjectives

    Much and many tell us something more about a noun. They are adjectives of quantity.
    We use much with uncountable nouns. We use many with plural nouns.

    Example:
  • There isn't much water in the bucket.
  • There aremany chairs in the room.

    • Possessive Adjectives

    My, our, his, her, its and their are possessive adjective. They are used with nouns to show that something belongs to somebody.

  • Is this your book

    • here your is a possessive adjective and bookis a noun. They are different from possessive pronouns. Possessive pronoun can stand alone but possessive adjectives are always placed to before nouns. They are placed in front of the nouns that they modify.

  • This is ours.

    • here ours is a possessive pronoun.

  • This is our flat.

    • here our is possessive adjective.

    Interrogative Adjectives

    An interrogative adjective (what, which, whose) is like an interrogative pronoun and is used to ask questions. however, unlike the interrogative pronoun, the interrogative adjective modifies a noun. What refers to the noun pencil and whose refers to the noun pen.


    Examples:


  • What colour is your shirt?
  • which pencil-box is yours?
  • Whose pen have you borrowed?
