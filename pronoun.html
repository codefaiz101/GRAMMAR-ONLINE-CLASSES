Pronoun

Pronouns are small words that take the place of a noun. Pronouns are words like: he , you , ours , themselves , some , each... we can use a pronoun instead of a noun. if we didn't have pronouns, we would have to repeat a whole lot of nouns.

Personal Pronoun
  • number:singular (e.g:I)or plural (e.g:we)
  • person: 1st person (e.g:I),2nd person(e.h:you)or 3rd person(e.g:he)
  • gender:male(e.g:he),female(e.g:she)or neuter(e.g:it)
  • case:subject(e.g:we)or object(e.g:us)

    • We use personal pronouns in place of the person or people that we are talking about. My name is josef but when i am talking about myself I almost always use"I" or "me" , not "josef". when i am talking direct to you , I almost always use "you" , not your name. when I am talking about another person, say john, I may start with "john" but then use "he' or "him".And so on.

    Demonstrative Pronoun

    A demonstrative pronoun represents a thing or things:

  • near in distance or time (this,these)
  • far in distance or time(that,those)

    • here are some examples with demonstrative pronoun , followed by an illustration:

  • This tastes good.
  • Have you seen this?
  • these are bad times.
  • Do you like these?

  • That is beutiful.
  • Look at that!
  • Those were the days!
  • can you see those?

  • This is heavier than that.
  • These are bigger than those.
    • Possessive Pronoun

    a pronoun indicating possession, for example mine, yours,here,theirs.
    Possessive pronoun include my, mine, our, ours, its, her, hers,their, theirs, your and yours.
    These are all words that demonstrate ownership. if the book belong to me, it is mine. if the book belongs to her, then it is here.

  • The cat is yours.
  • The kids are yours and mine.
  • The money was really theirs for the talking.
  • we shall finally have what is rightfully ours.
  • Their mother gets well with yours.
  • What's mine is yours, my friend.
  • The bag is theirs
    • Interrogative Pronouns

    We use interrogative pronoun to ask quetions. The interrogative pronoun represents the thing that we don't know . There are four main interrogative pronoun:who, whom, what, which

    Reflexive Pronouns

    We use a reflexive pronoun when we want to refer back to the subject of the sentense or clause.
    Reflexive pronouns end in "-self" (singular) or "-selves" (plural).

    The nine English Reflexive Pronounsare myself, yourself, himself, herself, oneself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, and themselves.

    Reciprocal Pronouns

    We use reciprocal pronoun when each of two or more subjects is acting in the same way towards the other. For example, A is talking to B, and B is talking to A. so we say :
    A and B are talking to each other.

    When to use reciprocal pronoun

  • There must be two or more people, things or groups reciprocal the action.
  • They must be performing the same action.
  • Reciprocal pronouns can not be used with i, you, he, she, and it because three must be multiple nouns or pronouna to reciprocate the action
    • Each Other:

    when the antecedent of a Reciprocal Pronoun refers to two things in a sentence, we use each other.

    Example Sentences
  • The two brothers always cooperate with each other.
  • kiran and karuna helped each other in the exam
  • Froends are kind to each other in case of need
  • The wife and husband love each other.

    • One Another:

    When the antecedent of a reciprocal Pronoun refers to more than two things in asentence, we use one another

    Example Sentences
  • The family members always quarrel with One another
  • The cpmpany directors fought one another
  • Froends are kind to one otherin casse of need
  • The employees started blaming one another
    • Indefinite Pronouns

    An indefinite pronoun does not refer to any specific person, thing or amount. it is vague and "not definite". some typical indefinite pronoun are:

  • all, another, any, anybody/anyone, anything, each, everybody/everything, few, many, nobody, none, one, several, some, some, somebody/someone.
    • Example sentence:
  • all: From my point of view, all are equal.
  • anybody: Anybody can dance on the floor of dancing.
  • every:i heartily congratulate every player on this team.
  • anything:i was unable to hear anything last night after the blast.
  • severalseveralpeople came to the stadium to see a match.

  • most:Mostof the student are participating in cultural events.
  • nothing:Nothingis left in front of me to tell you the truth.
  • no one:No onewill help you in this situation.

  • both:Get both out of my classroom.
    • Relative Pronouns

    A relative pronoun is a pronoun that introduce a relative clause. it is called a "relative" pronoun because it "relates" to the word that its relative clause modifies.

    For example: The person who phoned me last night is my teacher.

    There are five basic relative pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, that

    who and whom are generally only for people. whose is for possession. which is for things. That can be used for things and people only in defining relative clauses.
